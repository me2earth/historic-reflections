The itinerary, day 8

The Oceania Line's Riviera—a day at sea

Paraphrasing Robert Kunzig, in National Geographic; during the time between ports of call, you felt time and space passing differently than it does with other forms of travel, especially, "as you stepped onto the deck and the wind caught your body like a sail; felt it as you watched the foam part at the bow and rush along the sides. You watched the broad roiling wake disappear to the horizon…" Gradually one feels time slow down and become palpably more apparent, passing more distinctly than in the quotidian life back home.
Dinner at the Toscana Grill
We end our only full day at sea with dinner at Toscana Grill, no land in sight.
The scenery is beautiful. The sky and sunset that night put on a show as our dinner entertainment viewed through floor–to–ceiling glass. The sky and sunrises and sets were equally compelling other days as well.
Our day at sea closes with Fred and Bobbie's show in the main lounge, a comedic and visual delight of prestidigitation and theater. Their image is used with permission (visit fredbecker.com to learn more about their shows).
The Riviera is a beautiful mid–size ship (785 feet, 1,250 guests, 800 crew) that is a pleasure to spend time aboard—we consider it one of our vacation destinations. Amenities abound, service is ubiquitous and superlative, details delight, and activities are plentiful…
The artwork throughout the ship is worthy of a gallery and the attention to decorative details is impressive, including consummate guest service.

Each morning and evening we were warmly greeted in the hall outside our staterooms by our steward team who were always interested in what we had planned for the day, always cheerful delightful people. We would encounter them working their way up and down the hall so frequently that we began to speculate that they actually lived inside the ever–present cart that carried their tools and supplies, imagining a magically huge space inside the deceptively tiny exterior.
