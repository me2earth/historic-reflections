Historic Reflections 13, 14

The itinerary, days 13 to 14

Poseidon’s Temple
Rising early to another beautiful morning in Athens, NancyEllen hits a wall shortly after depositing her exhausted self into her tour bus seat, literally missing the whole thing except for a vague (accurate) assessment that Greek Mythology is a rich but weird confused jumble, featuring an extended dysfunctional pantheon of gods and goddesses, their human playthings, twisted sexual escapades, infidelity, demons, perversions, war, strife, more sex and lots of symbology. Our guide’s competent and comprehensive recitation of Greek mythology was fascinating to me, but I appeared to be in the minority—more than half the bus joined NancyEllen in a deep snooze on our trek to and from Poseidon’s Temple.

The coastline scenery was captivating and, to me, as engrossing as our guide’s stories—but difficult to capture through the bus windows.
